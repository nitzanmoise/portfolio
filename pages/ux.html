03.01.01 - Data collection

Focus

In the data collection assignment, we learned and practiced different ways to prepare an optimized user interface. Through these mathods we gattered information about the "target audiance" so we could make a firm faundation for a great user experiance.

Desk Research

The topic I chose for the desk research was the use of virtual reality in relation to elderly people with dementia. The idea of a desk research is to gather information from a collected data such as articles and media. Data collection allows the researcher to gain knowledge about a target audience/group and learn about recent trends and technology of the chosen topic.

Observasion

By using the observation method, I was able to collect data on different live situations. The focus here was to learn how to observe a “target audience”, take notes and notice the change of behaviour in different situations.

Interview

Interview as a primary research method helped me understand the experience and the opinion of experts in the topic. I also learned how important it is to ask open and deep questions so I would gather an effective information.

Survey

Survey is a unique research method because it is easy to collect information from many people by asking them the same questions. From the survey results we could understand that information changes between different groups of people. We could divide people into groups by asking for age and gender.

Feedbak and Improvement

The presentation I made was informative and followed the task description, but I could improve it by adding further depth both in collecting and processing the information. I could ask more elaborative questions so I could understand better the answers.

03.03.01 - Prototype

Goal

The goal of the prototype assignment was to make an optimal user experience prototype in adobe XD with an UI kit and a fully content production for the target audience. We also made a conventions research on the aforementioned platforms regarding design, content, and functionality, chose a tone of voice that will fit our massage for the target audience - formal, professional, respectful, yet enthusiastic.

My Part

My part in the group project was to make a style tile, producing and documenting micro copy and copy examples. I also conducted the wire-flow of our prototype in an easy flowing way from a landing page to a job finding page with a search bar and a filter to minimize the search results.

Testing

At last we conducted a think aloud test to test our prototype and see how we can improve our user interface by the experience of our testers. We asked the testers to follow few steps in our prototype and say what they are seeing, and what they should do next. In the end of the test we asked some questions and we reviewed the answers from the transcription of the tests. We learned that our prototype was simple and easy to review, yet we could optimize our search bar filter to be more specific.

03.04.01 - Design sprint

Focus

In the design sprint, it was the first time we had to work in a large group on an intense project. Our goal was to build in five days a native app prototype while each day had a different professional focus. The focus on each day was based of what we learned in the theme. We had to practice our skills and expand our knowledge in ux msthods such as: data collection, information architacture, content production, testing methods and more...

My part

From the begnning of the sprint I was very dedicated to find a real problem that we could solve within our app concept. I came up with this problem - many new students in KEA find it difficult to socialize and make new friendships. After mapping and brainstorming different long-term goals I suggested a solution of an app where students would be able to create events and activities and invite their friends from school. Furthermore, I designed a concept map, and came up with some of the questions for the “experts” interviews”. I organized the story board from the chosen flow of our sketches, added more potential pages and functionality. I was a maker and as a part of the role I documented what we made so far and helped with the improvement of the prototype. In the pitch day I talked about the prototype itself and showed the flow of the app.

Achievements

Through the sprint I learned the meaning of having different roles in a group and how important the roles are. When we didn’t work with the roles it was hard for us to agree and progress. On the fourth day of the sprint I decided to become a facilitator. I made sure that everyone know what to do and what is their role – who’s making the prototype and who’s writing the content. Through my role as a facilitator I gained understanding of how a group should function in a design sprint with specific roles and an up to date schedule.

Feedback

The first major feedback point we got after our pitch was that even tough our app called “KEA Social” there is not enough details that connecting the platform to KEA, in the design and the layout. The second point was that we had a large "build-up" in our pitch for our platform but when we showed the prototype, we didn’t have enough time to show the whole flow and functionality.
